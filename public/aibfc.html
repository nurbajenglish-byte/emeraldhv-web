Questions

Everything you need before you register.

Who can enter?

Any student in Grades 7–12, from any school, in any country. Financial Literacy is for Grades 7–9; Entrepreneurship for Grades 10–12; the Portfolio Pathway is open to all grades.

Do students need a finance background?

No. This is the first edition and it’s built to be beginner-friendly. Every registration includes free practice exams, study sets and games so a motivated first-timer can prepare from scratch.

What does the qualifying exam look like?

Both tracks share the same format: 40 questions in one 60-minute sitting, taken online on 17–18 October. Results and finalist invitations follow on 29–30 October.

What do Portfolio Pathway students receive?

One week of live online lessons from leading experts, plus an individual, verifiable credential built to strengthen a university application — on top of everything in the team tracks.

Do we have to travel?

The qualifying exam on 17–18 October is online — sit it from your own school. Only those who qualify travel, to the international finals in Hong Kong, 4–6 December.

What does every participant get?

An official certificate. Finalists additionally earn distinction certificates, medals and trophies, plus written feedback from the judging panel.

How and when do we pay?

Registration opens 1 July 2026 and is paid securely online by card. Early-bird pricing ($25 per team, all tracks) runs through 1 September; standard pricing ($30, all tracks) applies from 2 September.

Can one teacher register several teams?

Yes. A school coordinator can register multiple teams in bulk and manage them from one account. Email us to be set up as a coordinator.

What is the refund policy?

Full refunds are available until 10 October 2026 — one week before the qualifying exam. After that date, registration fees are non-refundable, as exam logistics are committed based on entry counts. If A-IBFC 2026 is cancelled by the organisers, all fees are refunded in full.
